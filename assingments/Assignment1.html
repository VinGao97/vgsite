Reading Response

Websites are an extension of the creator. The content and styling is at the will of the webmaster. They are essentially the architechs of the internet. We, the creators use our knowledge to create a home for out content that is the website itself. Laurel talks about the landscape of the interenet and how the content and the functionality of a website could be anything. There is an endless possiblity to what a website could be. Having a website is also very important to an individual. To an artiest, the website works side by side to their artworks. The website will become the house/gallery to their creations. To a normal person it could become a portfolio to their works and displays anything interesting that they might want to share.

Laurel uses a room and various objects inside the room to compare to a website and its contents. The contents are what decorates the room and give it its characteristic and functionality. The metaphors she uses connects to us on a personal level because we all know what a room or a garden or a puddle looks like, she just uses these connections to make coding a website seem like something you can shape yourself.
